Old Dubai: Where It All Began

Before the towering skyscrapers and luxury hotels, Dubai was a humble trading port built along Dubai Creek. Today, you can still experience this authentic heritage in the Al Fahidi Historical Neighborhood, where traditional wind-tower architecture and narrow alleyways transport you back in time. Take an abra (water taxi) across the creek for just 1 dirham and explore the gold, spice, and textile souks where locals still shop daily.

Dubai Desert Conservation Reserve

Experience protected desert ecosystems, spot Arabian oryx and gazelles, and learn about Bedouin traditions in this 225 square kilometer natural reserve - a side of Dubai few tourists discover.
